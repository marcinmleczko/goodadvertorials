— The Thesis

Most DTC brands run segmented ads to one generic landing page. The promise doesn't match the page, the page doesn't match the reader, and the reader doesn't convert. The fix is older than the internet: an editorial presell that meets the ad on its own terms, then escorts the reader to the offer. We build those. — How These Convert

Five Reasons These Pages Beat The Control.

Not theory. What's actually moving CVR in 2026.

  1. 01

    Editorial presell, not product page.

    Cold traffic can't handle a product page. It needs an editorial-feeling presell that lowers skepticism and raises belief before the offer. Every page here is a listicle or longform advertorial. Every one.

  2. 02

    Message match from ad to CTA.

    The ad angle, the headline, the hook, the proof, the CTA—all the same thread. Congruence is the quiet 20% lift most brands leave on the table.

  3. 03

    Proof density on every scroll.

    Stats, testimonials, before/after, press logos, FAQs, risk reversal. A reader should hit proof every three hundred pixels. Belief stacks.

  4. 04

    One CTA, repeated until the end.

    One offer. One button copy. Five to seven placements through the page. No "learn more" links bleeding intent to other routes.

  5. 05

    Shipped in days, iterated weekly.

    An AI-assisted build stack on top of tested master templates. V1 live in three days, V2 shipped after the first hundred thousand impressions. That cadence is the edge.

— Frequently Asked

Common Questions.

What exactly do you build?

Presell pages, advertorials, listicles, and longform editorial pages for DTC brands running paid traffic. Not product pages. Not full site rebuilds.

How fast is fast?

V1 live in three to five working days after brief and assets. Revisions in hours. Most brands ship two to three variants in the first two weeks of a test.

Do I own the page?

Yes. It's built on your Shopify theme, or wherever you host. No lock-in, no platform, no monthly fee for access.

How do we measure whether it worked?

A/B test against your current control. If the new page doesn't beat it on the primary metric you pick (usually CVR or CPA), the build is free.

What does it cost?

Depends on scope and traffic volume. Fixed fee per page or performance rev-share. Both options on the intro call.
